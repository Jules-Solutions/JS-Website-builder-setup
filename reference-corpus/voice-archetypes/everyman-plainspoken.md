# Plainspoken Everyman

> Plain, honest, and inclusive — it says exactly what it means in words everyone already knows.

## Voice description

The Plainspoken Everyman is the anti-jargon voice. It treats clarity as respect: no buzzwords, no inflation, no insider vocabulary that leaves anyone out. It's friendly but not performative, confident but not boastful, and it sounds like a straight-talking, decent person explaining something to a neighbor. It wins trust by being obviously honest — it'll tell you the trade-offs and the price without games. This is the register of practical retail, home services, utilities, civic/government, no-nonsense tools, and value brands. The feeling it leaves is "no catch — they just tell it to me straight."

## Framework grounding

- **NN4D profile:** neutral-humor / casual / respectful / matter-of-fact. Deliberately central on most axes — the Everyman's power is *not* having a strong stylistic flavor; it's relatable precisely because it doesn't perform. Casual + respectful = approachable without pandering.
- **Aaker — Sincerity** (down-to-earth, honest, wholesome). The IKEA posture: plain, inclusive, unpretentious, good value stated plainly.
- **Jung — Everyman** (core drive: belonging, realism; exemplar IKEA). The Everyman's built-in never is *elitist* — it never talks down, shows off vocabulary, or makes the reader feel like an outsider.

## Attributes

- **Primary:** plain, honest, clear, inclusive, unpretentious.
- **Flex range:** can warm toward "friendly neighbor" for community copy; can firm toward "practical/no-nonsense" for specs and policies. Never flexes into luxury formality, hype, or irony.

## Say / never-say

- **Say:** everyday words, short sentences, honest trade-offs, plain prices, "here's how it works," "no, that won't work for you — try this instead." Translate every necessary technical term.
- **Never say:** jargon ("synergize," "leverage," "best-in-class"), inflated claims, fake exclusivity, or anything that assumes special knowledge. If your grandparent wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.

## Sample copy

- **Hero:** *"Flat-pack furniture that's easy to build and easy to afford. Pick it up today, or we'll deliver it Saturday."*
- **CTA:** *"See what's in stock"* / *"Find your nearest store"*
- **Microcopy (shipping note):** *"Delivery is £4.95, or free if you spend over £50. No surprises at checkout — the price you see is the price you pay."*

## Best for / avoid for

- **Best for:** practical retail, home & trades services, utilities, civic/government, value brands, everyday tools, anything whose edge is honesty and accessibility.
- **Avoid for:** luxury (too plain to signal premium), brands competing on prestige or cutting-edge innovation, or audiences who specifically want expert-grade depth.
