# Warm Guide

> An encouraging, reassuring expert who walks beside you — knowledgeable, but never above you.

## Voice description

The Warm Guide combines competence with care. It knows its subject cold but leads with empathy: it anticipates the reader's worry and answers it before it's asked. Sentences are conversational and second-person, the tone is encouraging without being saccharine, and confidence is offered to the reader rather than claimed for the brand. This is the register of healthcare, wellness, coaching, education, financial guidance for nervous beginners, and any service where the customer feels uncertain or vulnerable. The feeling it leaves is "I'm in good hands and they actually care."

## Framework grounding

- **NN4D profile:** neutral-humor / casual / respectful / enthusiastic. Enthusiasm here is *quiet warmth*, not loudness — "we're glad you're here," not "LET'S GO!!!" Casual-but-respectful keeps it from tipping into either clinical or patronizing.
- **Aaker — Sincerity** (down-to-earth, honest, wholesome, cheerful), with a borrowed sub-trait of Competence (reliable) so the warmth is backed by authority. Patagonia-ish trustworthiness, softened toward care.
- **Jung — Caregiver** (core drive: nurturing, protection; exemplar J&J). The Caregiver's built-in never is *cold* — it never makes the reader feel judged, rushed, or stupid.

## Attributes

- **Primary:** warm, reassuring, clear, encouraging.
- **Flex range:** can steady toward "calm expert" for clinical accuracy on detail pages; can brighten toward "friendly" for community/welcome copy. Never flexes into hype or sarcasm.

## Say / never-say

- **Say:** "you," "we'll help you," "here's what to expect," "it's normal to feel…," plain reassurance backed by specifics. Name the worry, then resolve it.
- **Never say:** clinical jargon without translation, anything that implies the reader should already know something, fear-based pressure, or fake cheerfulness ("Amazing news!!!"). Respect over rah-rah.

## Sample copy

- **Hero:** *"Therapy that fits your life. Match with a licensed therapist who gets you, and have your first session this week — from wherever you feel comfortable."*
- **CTA:** *"Find your therapist"* / *"See how it works"*
- **Microcopy (form helper):** *"No rush — you can change your answers anytime. This just helps us match you with someone who's a good fit."*

## Best for / avoid for

- **Best for:** healthcare, wellness, therapy, coaching, education, nonprofits, family services, beginner-facing finance.
- **Avoid for:** luxury (reads as soft), hard-edged performance brands, or B2B audiences who want signal over reassurance.
