# Bold Motivator

> Direct, second-person, action-first — it believes in you and won't let you coast.

## Voice description

The Bold Motivator writes like a great coach mid-session: short, hard-hitting lines, imperatives, and an unshakeable belief that the reader can do the thing. It's serious about the goal but high-energy about the pursuit. Sentences are command-shaped ("Show up. Do the work."), second person dominates, and specifics (numbers, outcomes) keep it from being empty hype. The confidence is transferred to the reader, not hoarded by the brand. This is the register of fitness, sports, performance, productivity, and bootstrappy "go build it" brands. The feeling it leaves is "let's go — I can actually do this."

## Framework grounding

- **NN4D profile:** serious / casual / respectful / enthusiastic. Serious-and-enthusiastic together is the signature: it cares deeply (serious) and brings heat (enthusiastic). Casual keeps it human; respectful keeps it from drill-sergeant cruelty.
- **Aaker — Excitement** (daring, spirited) with a **Ruggedness** borrow (tough, no-frills). The Nike posture: bold, motivating, decisive.
- **Jung — Hero** (core drive: courage, mastery, triumph; exemplar Nike). The Hero's built-in never is *timid* — it never hedges, apologizes, or softens the challenge into a suggestion.

## Attributes

- **Primary:** direct, energetic, motivating, confident, decisive.
- **Flex range:** can ground toward "rugged/no-frills" for gear and durability claims; can warm slightly for community/beginner copy so it inspires rather than intimidates. Never flexes into hedging or calm minimalism.

## Say / never-say

- **Say:** imperatives ("Start today." "Add weight."), second person, specific numbers and milestones, momentum verbs. One exclamation max — let the verbs carry the energy, not the punctuation.
- **Never say:** hedging ("you might want to consider…"), apology, wellness-y calm, corporate filler. Don't be mean — confident, never cruel. The reader is an athlete, not a target.

## Sample copy

- **Hero:** *"Stronger every week. No fluff, no machines you'll never use — just the work that moves the needle. Your first session is on us."*
- **CTA:** *"Start training"* / *"Claim your first week"*
- **Microcopy (progress nudge):** *"Three sessions down. Don't break the streak — your next workout takes 35 minutes."*

## Best for / avoid for

- **Best for:** fitness, sports, performance gear, productivity tools, coaching with an edge, bootstrapper/maker brands.
- **Avoid for:** luxury (too loud), anxious or vulnerable audiences (reads as pressure), enterprise B2B, or anything where calm credibility outsells energy.
