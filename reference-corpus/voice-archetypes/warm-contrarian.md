# Warm Contrarian

> Takes a real stance against how its industry does things — but stays kind, human, and on the reader's side.

## Voice description

The Warm Contrarian has a thesis. It believes the category is broken in some specific way, and it says so plainly — then offers a better way without arrogance. What separates it from the Playful Challenger is warmth and sincerity: it's not joking, it's not punching down, it genuinely cares and it's a little fed up on the reader's behalf. It picks a fight with *practices*, not people. This is the register of mission-driven brands, ethical alternatives, opinionated software (Basecamp-shaped), and B2C/B2B disruptors who lead with values. The feeling it leaves is "finally, someone who gets why this is broken — and they're decent about it."

## Framework grounding

- **NN4D profile:** neutral-to-funny / casual / irreverent-but-kind / enthusiastic. The "irreverent-but-kind" reading of the respect dimension is the whole identity: it breaks convention (irreverent) without contempt (kind). Enthusiasm comes from conviction, not volume.
- **Aaker — Sincerity** (honest, down-to-earth) fused with **Excitement** (daring, spirited). The Patagonia-with-an-edge posture: trustworthy values delivered with nerve.
- **Jung — Outlaw tempered by Caregiver.** Outlaw (revolution, disruption; exemplar Harley-Davidson) supplies the stance; Caregiver supplies the warmth so the defiance never curdles into cynicism. Built-in nevers: *compliant* (Outlaw) and *cold* (Caregiver).

## Attributes

- **Primary:** principled, candid, warm, spirited, human.
- **Flex range:** can sharpen toward "manifesto" for values/about pages; can soften toward "warm guide" for support and onboarding. Never flexes into corporate neutrality or mean-spirited snark.

## Say / never-say

- **Say:** a clear stance ("Most CRMs are built to trap you. We're not."), "here's what we believe," honest critique of the status quo aimed at practices, plain language, conviction. Name the problem, take the side of the reader.
- **Never say:** smug superiority, contempt for customers who chose competitors, performative outrage, or values-washing with no substance behind it. Take a stance, never a swipe.

## Sample copy

- **Hero:** *"Project tools shouldn't need a 12-week onboarding and a 'success manager.' Ours works on day one — because you have actual work to do, not software to learn."*
- **CTA:** *"Try it free — no demo call"* / *"Read what we believe"*
- **Microcopy (pricing note):** *"One price. No 'contact sales,' no per-seat creep, no annual lock-in. If it stops being worth it, you leave. That's the deal."*

## Best for / avoid for

- **Best for:** mission/values-led brands, ethical alternatives, opinionated software, sustainability, independent makers, disruptors positioning against a bloated incumbent.
- **Avoid for:** conservative enterprise buyers who distrust opinion, neutral utilities, luxury, or any brand without a real, defensible stance — a faked contrarian voice is transparent and corrodes trust.
