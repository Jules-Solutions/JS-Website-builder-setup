# Refined Luxe

> Restrained, sensory, and unhurried — it lets silence and craft do the persuading.

## Voice description

The Refined Luxe voice sells by withholding. It never shouts a discount or stacks adjectives; it states one beautiful thing plainly and trusts it to land. Copy is sparse, rhythmically controlled, and sensory — it evokes material, light, and feeling rather than listing features. Formality signals exclusivity; matter-of-factness signals that the quality is self-evident and needs no defending. This is the register of luxury fashion, fine hospitality, premium spirits, high-end design, and craft makers. The feeling it leaves is "this is for people with taste, and the price is not the point."

## Framework grounding

- **NN4D profile:** serious / formal / respectful / matter-of-fact. The matter-of-fact + formal pairing is essential — enthusiasm would read as trying too hard, which is the death of luxury. Confidence is shown through restraint.
- **Aaker — Sophistication** (upper-class, glamorous, charming, romantic). The luxury-editorial posture: refined, restrained, elevated. May borrow a Lover's sensory intimacy.
- **Jung — Lover / Ruler blend.** Lover (passion, intimacy, sensory devotion) for the craft and feeling; Ruler (control, premium leadership; exemplar Mercedes) for the composure. Built-in nevers: *clinical* (Lover) and *chaotic* (Ruler) — never sterile, never cluttered.

## Attributes

- **Primary:** elegant, restrained, sensory, composed, assured.
- **Flex range:** can warm toward "intimate" for personal/atelier storytelling; can formalize toward "heritage authority" for legacy claims. Never flexes into playful, urgent, or discount-driven.

## Say / never-say

- **Say:** concrete sensory nouns (linen, oak, dusk), short evocative lines, understatement, provenance and craft details stated once. Let white space breathe; one strong sentence over three.
- **Never say:** "sale," "limited time," exclamation marks, adjective stacks ("luxurious, premium, exquisite, exclusive"), feature bullet-spam, or anything that bargains. Urgency cheapens; restraint elevates.

## Sample copy

- **Hero:** *"Made in Florence, by hand, in numbers small enough to count. A bag meant to outlast the season — and most of what you'll buy this decade."*
- **CTA:** *"Discover the collection"* / *"Book a private viewing"*
- **Microcopy (product detail):** *"Vegetable-tanned leather, finished over six weeks. It will darken where you hold it. That is the point."*

## Best for / avoid for

- **Best for:** luxury fashion & accessories, fine hospitality, premium spirits/wine, high-end interiors, jewelry, craft makers, private services.
- **Avoid for:** mass-market value brands, anything price-led, beginner/anxious audiences (reads as cold or exclusionary), or fast-moving promotional retail.
